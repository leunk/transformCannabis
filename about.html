Who We Are

Transform Cannabis is a team of individuals who promote the education of cannabis for those who are willing to learn. Our main goal is we want people to know the truth about cannabis regardless of the societal stigma regarding Cannabis.

Vision & Mission

The mission of Transform Cannabis is to educate Floridians and Americans about the different resources that the Cannabis plant has to offer. As well as, advocating for bill SB 1860, allowing for Florida adults to consume state regulated cannabis.

Overview of the History of Cannabis

Cannabis can first be noticed around 500 BC used in ancient cultures as a medicinal herb.

The first instance of cannabis in America was when our early colonial ancestors brought over hemp from the motherland. The main use of the hemp cultivated at this time was used for ropes and fabric.

Currently in the 20th century cannabis has taken a negative form in the United States due to the criminalization done in 1937 and the racial factors that had been implemented heavily during the 1970's when the war on drugs was declared. Although cannabis is becoming legal in different states across the nation, cannabis is still a schedule 1 drug therefore is federally illegal.

Cannabis is a fast-growing plant that is generally a painless plant to cultivate which is why cannabis is so widely available in many different countries. Cannabis is hypothesized to originate in Asia that then was spread across Africa, then made its way to Europe, and finally made its way to America.

Cannabis is composed of different chemicals, the main two are tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) the psychoactive chemical that gives users a euphoric sense after use and cannabidiol (CBD) the non-intoxicating chemical produced by the plant.

History of Marijuana Prohibition

The history of Marijuana prohibition in the United States begins around the early 1900's shortly after the Mexican Revolution. After the conclusion of the Mexican Revolution, many immigrants made their way to the United States. With the surge of new Americans entering the country, the public had been overly concerned about their new fellow Americans. This concern influenced the media to utilize scare tactics to paint Mexican-Americans in a dangerous and disruptive light. This led to the exoneration of Mexicans and Mexican-Americans. The media succeeded in scaring the public into thinking that "marihuana", how Mexicans referred to cannabis.

With the public fearing Mexican-Americans and the media spreading false attributes it was no problem for the public and fellow Americans to demonize cannabis in conjunction with demonizing Mexicans and Mexican-Americans. Texan citizens were stalking their new neighbors and finding ways to control them to their benefit. Texans figured that if they could find a way to search, detain, and deport their new neighbors, that they would end up doing more good on behalf of the country.

Marijuana was the way Texan citizens were able to control the Mexican immigrant population in the United States.

In the 1930's there was a hearing for the marijuana law. In this hearing claims were made that "marijuana has the ability to cause men of color to become violent and solicit sex from white women." By the end of the hearing the Marihuana Tax Act of 1937 was born and successfully forbade the use and sales of marijuana. The Marihuana Tax Act was deemed unconstitutional several years after it had been enacted.
